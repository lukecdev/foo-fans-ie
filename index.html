History of the Band

Band History

Foo Fighters are an American rock band that was formed in Seattle, Washington, in 1994. The band was founded by Dave Grohl, who was the drummer for the world-renowned rock band Nirvana following the dissolution of the band. Foo Fighters was originally a one-man project to record his own songs that he wrote while on tours with Nirvana. This initial demo album was handed out to his friends under the temporary name “Foo Fighters”. The band was originally formed to support the album and they made their public debut on February 23, 1995, in California at the Jambalaya Club. Since then, the band has released 10 studio albums. They have collected 15 Grammy Awards making them one of the most successful rock acts in Grammy history. In 2021 the band was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, in their first year of eligibility.
